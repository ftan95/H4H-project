Consulting as a Career


What Consultants Do

Aim to improve an organization's position or profile by helping to solve problems, manage change and improve efficiency. Gives expert advice to companies and individuals.

Fast Facts & Info

Number of Hours per week: 55-70 Average starting pay: $80k-110k Difficulty level to enter field: hard Amount of Travel required: weekly.

Addtional Resources & Training

Books for interview: Case in Point, Case Interview Secrets, 20 Days to Ace the Case, Mastering the Case Interview Additional resources: on Bain, BCG, and McKinsey Website.

Career Exit Options

Corporations: Operations Management, Product Management, Strategy, etc. Private Equity or Venture Capital Startups.

Necessary Skills & Steps to Enter Field

Network with professionals (often need to get references for firms, especially from nontarget schools) Practice case interviews - almost all firms use this as part of their recruitment If possible, get consulting experience (through consulting clubs, volunteering, classes, etc.)

Top Firms and Landscape of Industry

MBB (top firms): McKinsey, Bain, Boston Consulting Group (BCG) Big 4 (accounting firms that have advisory practices): Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG. PwC and EY have "strategy" sides, Strategy& and Parthenon, respectively. Other Major Firms (Tier 2): Accenture, AT Kearney, Booz Allen Hamilton, Oliver Wyman, LEK Boutique firms: highly specialized and/or small consulting firms. Many of these throughout U.S. and world
